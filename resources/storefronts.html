Digital Storefronts

Consider the difference between "existing on a larger site that advertises for you but you must bend to their whims" (Etsy, RedBubble, etc), and "having your own site you have to advertise yourself" (most others). People often have an Etsy to find new people, and then send a business card that gives their personal storefront info to order from in the future.

Ko-Fi

A very popular website for receiving tips (donations), commissions, and monthly memberships. You can provide any service, physical or digital. There is a gallery option for your page, and you can sell fixed digital content there as well. They take a 5% fee, or 0% if you pay for their premium membership (it's also 0% on donations for everyone, though you still get hit with Paypal/Stripe fees). They do not allow pornography, nudity, obscene, or violent content.

Big Cartel

A custom storefront where you pay a set monthly fee instead of transaction fees: $0 for 5 products, and it goes up from there. You can sell physical goods, digital goods, and even do art commissions through it. It just is done in a more hands-on way - emailing the client after they pay you to get the details, or requesting they email you. They allow NSFW content, though censor it in your storefront display.

Etsy

Allows you to sell physical and digital content, along with commissions. There is a $0.20 fee for listing an item, and a 6.5% transaction fee. You can pay to promote yourself on the site. Full fee breakdown. They allow sexually explicit content but it must be censored on the site itself, and no pornography (mature content policy).

Print on Demand Services

These services will take your art and print it on different items, and either ship it back to you or to your client. The main difference is that they do not have a shopfront for you and do not advertise for you. You use your own storefront.
